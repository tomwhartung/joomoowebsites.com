Finely Crafted Sites

Technical work always takes longer than we think it will take.

More than that, I like to take pride in my work. The fact that quality is a subjective term makes things more confusing.

This does not mean I am a perfectionist, only that I care.

Some people want quick-and-dirty results, but are afraid to admit they are not interested in quality work. I call quick-and-dirty work prototyping. If you are cool with me cutting corners, call it prototyping, so I know in advance it will be wasteful making something nice because it's just going to wind up in the trash.

Does that make sense?

Prototyping

Not sure what you want? I have a basic structure that makes it easy to put together a simple site.

It's a cinch to move it to wordpress, react, drupal, or whatever later, if that's what you decide you want to do.

My inclination is to take my time and craft something nice. If you want something quick and dirty, let me know in advance. This will let me know I can accept a lower price than if I was going to craft something nice.

Incremental Projects and Payments

KISS has always been a rule.

Things change too quickly.

I am embracing the churn.

I do not want to do a bunch of work for someone only to have them refuse to pay. If we are going to work together, we will need to establish trust. The best way to do that is incrementally, with small projects.
